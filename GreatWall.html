Great Wall of China

  • Location: Northern China, stretching over 13,000 miles.
  • Construction: Built between the 7th century BCE and the 17th century CE by multiple dynasties.
  • Purpose: Originally constructed to protect against invasions from northern tribes.
  • Materials: Made from earth, wood, bricks, and stone, depending on the region.
  • Features: Includes watchtowers, battlements, and beacon towers.
  • Modern Significance: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Chinese history and culture.
