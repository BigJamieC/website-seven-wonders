Christ the Redeemer

  • Location: Mount Corcovado, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
  • Height: Stands 30 meters (98 feet) tall, with a 28-meter (92 feet) arm span.
  • Material: Made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.
  • Construction: Completed in 1931 and designed by French sculptor Paul Landowski.
  • Symbolism: Represents peace, Christianity, and Brazil's openness to visitors.
  • Modern Recognition: One of the New Seven Wonders of the World and an iconic landmark.
