Machu Picchu

  • Location: Andes Mountains, near Cusco, Peru.
  • Discovery: Rediscovered in 1911 by American explorer Hiram Bingham.
  • Civilization: Built by the Inca Empire around the 15th century.
  • Purpose: Likely served as a royal estate, ceremonial site, or retreat for Incan rulers.
  • Architecture: Features impressive stone masonry, terraced fields, and temples.
  • Main Attractions: The Temple of the Sun, the Intihuatana Stone, and the Room of the Three Windows.
  • Altitude: Located at approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level.
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site since 1983 and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Modern Tourism: A popular destination and symbol of Incan ingenuity and resilience.
